My Projects in New York City

I'm Pietro, an Italian civil engineer and content creator living in New York City. Besides filming raw street walks and daily life, I'm also building real projects here: a community-driven coffee shop, a Web3 real estate platform, and creative collaborations with local businesses.

This page is the place where everything comes together — the story behind BlockYork FoodHall, PropertyDEX and my photo & video collaborations across the city.

Discover BlockYork FoodHall

BlockYork FoodHall

Community-Driven Coffee Shop

BlockYork FoodHall is one of the most exciting projects I'm working on: a coffee shop concept in New York City that is born together with a community, not just a traditional business from the top down.

The idea is simple: create a real place in NYC for people who love the city, enjoy food, and are curious about innovation. A physical spot where the community can meet, connect and feel part of something bigger.

Behind the scenes, BlockYork FoodHall is supported by blockchain and tokenization, but the heart of the project is human: people, stories, and shared experiences.

Built with the Community

My goal is not just to open "another coffee shop", but to create one of the first community-driven coffee concepts in New York City.

People who follow my walks, love NYC, or are interested in Web3 and real estate can be part of this journey — from the early story to future steps of the project.

It's a cultural project, a tech project and a social project at the same time.

PropertyDEX

Web3 Real Estate, Built from New York City

PropertyDEX is my long-term mission in the real estate and Web3 space. As a civil engineer, I've always been fascinated by how buildings, cities and investments work — and how they could be more transparent and accessible.

Since 2017 I've been studying blockchain and tokenization. PropertyDEX is where that journey takes shape: a platform designed to connect people, projects and regulated partners in the world of real estate tokenization.

The focus is not on hype or speculation, but on building a solid, legal and compliant ecosystem together with certified companies that operate within real regulations.

In the future, BlockYork FoodHall and other community projects will also connect to the PropertyDEX ecosystem, creating a bridge between real places in New York City and on-chain ownership models.
